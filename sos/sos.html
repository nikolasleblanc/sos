The Pill

You took a pill less than 48 hours ago. Your contraception should still be effective.

  • Step 1

  • Take one active pill as soon as possible and continue taking one pill daily until the end of the pack.

  • Note

  • The pill is most effective when it is taken at the same time every day. If you have trouble remembering to consistently take your pill, perhaps you should consider a different method of contraception.

The Pill

You forgot to take one or more of your pills, or took a pill over 48 hours ago.

  • Step 1

  • Take one active pill as soon as possible and continue taking one pill daily until the end of the pack.

  • Step 2

  • If you had unprotected sexual intercourse in the last 5 days and are concerned about an unplanned pregnancy, emergency contracpetion ("the morning after pill" or IUD) is recommended and should be used as soon as possible.

    If you wish to discuss your emergency contraception options, consult your local pharmacist, nurse, or physician.

    Emergency contraception is effective for up to 5 days after unprotected sexual intercourse.

  • Step 3

  • You should also use back-up contraception, such as condoms, for the next 7 days.

  • Note

  • The pill is most effective when it is taken at the same time every day. If you have trouble remembering to consistently take your pill, perhaps you should consider a different method of contraception.

The Shot

You've had unprotected sex in the past 5 days. Contact your health-care professional. Take a pregnancy test.

If the pregnancy test is negative:

  • Step 1

  • If you had unprotected sexual intercourse in the last 5 days and are concerned about an unplanned pregnancy, emergency contraception ("the morning after pill" or IUD) is recommended and should be used as soon as possible.

    If you wish to discuss your emergency contraception options, consult your local pharmacist, nurse, or physician.

    Emergency contraception is effective for up to 5 days after unprotected sexual intercourse.

  • Step 2

  • You should have your next injection as soon as possible.

  • Step 3

  • You should use back-up contraception such as condoms, for the next 7 days after your injection.

  • Step 4

  • You should see your health-care professional and take another pregnancy test in 3 weeks.

  • Note

  • Note: The injection is most effective when it is administered every 12 to 13 weeks on a regular schedule.

    If you cannot see a health-care professional every 12 to 13 weeks for your injection, perhaps you should consider a different method of contraception.

If the pregnancy test is positive and confirms that you are pregnant:

  • Step 1

  • It is important to speak with a health-care professional or contact a medical clinic as soon as possible, to discuss whether you plan to continue the pregnancy or not.
